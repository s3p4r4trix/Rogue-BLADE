# Rogue:BLADE Hardware Naming Compendium

This document serves as the master list for all in-game hardware components. The naming convention follows a combined approach: [Manufacturer/Brand/Origin] + [Functional Description].

## 1. Anti-Grav Engines

Mechanic: Controls Speed, Acoustic Stealth, Energy Consumption, and Evasion (Flux).

* The All-Rounder (Basic): Kuro-Tech "Drifter" Mag-Lev
> Lore: Cheap, reliable, mass-produced. Good balance, but excels at nothing.

* The Heavy Lifter (Tank): Atlas "Hauler" Grav-Drive
> Lore: Slow and consumes a lot of energy, but absolutely necessary to lift Tier III materials like Neutronium.

* The Speed Demon (High Speed): Apex "Screamer" Ion-Thruster
> Lore: Insanely fast linear speed, but incredibly loud. Wakes up every alien within a mile.

* The Acrobat (Evasion/Flux): Dynacorp Vector-Shift Impeller
> Lore: Specializes in rapid, physics-defying directional changes. Perfect for parrying and dodging.

* The Ninja (Silent/Stealth): Aegis "Ghost" Whisper-Drive
> Lore: Emits zero decibels and masks thermal output. Essential for ambush routines.

* The Alien Artifact (Ultimate): Zenith "Anomaly" Void-Step Core
> Lore: Reverse-engineered alien tech. Moves so fast it appears to teleport, bypassing standard physics entirely.

## 2. Energy Cells

Mechanic: Controls Total Capacity, Combat Regeneration Rate, and Max Burst Output.

* Basic / Scavenged: Scrap-Built Dynamo Cell
> Lore: Barely holds a charge, constantly leaks minimal energy.

* High Capacity (Endurance): Voltaic "Long-Haul" Battery
> Lore: Huge energy pool, but very slow to recharge once depleted. Great for long levels.

* Fast Regeneration: Surge "Flash-Charge" Capacitor
> Lore: Small total pool, but refills almost instantly. Perfect for constant, low-energy actions.

* High Output (Burst): Hyperion Plasma-Core
> Lore: Required to power energy-hungry attachments like Plasma Blades or Cloaking Fields.

* Alien / Ultimate: Zenith Dark-Matter Isotope
> Lore: A virtually infinite energy source, pulsating with cold alien light.

## 3. Sensors

Mechanic: Unlocks specific "IF" Triggers in the Gambit UI.

* Organic Detection: Vital-Scan "Pulse" Biosensor
> Lore: Tracks heartbeats and bio-rhythms. Useless against pure drones.

* Shield/Synth Detection: Spectra EM-Scanner
> Lore: Highlights electromagnetic fields, alien shields, and synthetic enemies.

* Long-Range Tracking: Echo "Sweeper" Radar
> Lore: Standard military wide-area tracking. Blinded by chaff.

* Precision Targeting: Oculus Pinpoint Lidar
> Lore: Required for critical-strike routines. Blinded by smoke/fog.

* Wall-Piercing: Phantom Terahertz Array
> Lore: Sees through solid concrete. Perfect for targeting enemies behind cover.

## 4. Blades & Edges

Mechanic: Determines Damage Type and Armor Penetration.

* Standard Slash: Carbon "Razor" Edge
> Lore: A standard, highly sharpened rim. Good against unarmored targets.

* Blunt/Crushing: Titan "Breaker" Profile
> Lore: A thickened, blunt edge designed to shatter bones and dent heavy mechs through sheer kinetic force.

* Armor Saw: Resonance Vibro-Blade
> Lore: Teeth that vibrate at ultrasonic speeds. Eats through medium armor but draws continuous power.

* Ultimate Piercing: Hyper-Thread Monofilament Wire
> Lore: A wire one molecule thick spanning the edge. Slices anything, but breaks easily.

* Shield Melter: Ignis Plasma-Edge
> Lore: A ring of contained plasma. Melts Zenith shields instantly, but drains cells rapidly.

## 5. Form Designs (Chassis Shape)

Mechanic: Dictates the overall combat specialization.

* The Jack-of-all-Trades: Mk1 "Striker" Disc
> Lore: The classic Shuriken shape. Perfectly balanced.

* The Piercer: Aero "Viper" Dagger
> Lore: A sleek, dart-like chassis meant for high-speed, linear thrust attacks.

* The Crusher: Juggernaut Sphere
> Lore: A heavy, solid ball of death. Terrible aerodynamics, unmatched blunt force.

* The Energy Specialist: Halo Energy-Ring
> Lore: A hollowed-out chassis designed specifically to house unstable plasma loops.

## 6. Hull Materials (Lore-Tiered)

Mechanic: Determines Base HP, Weight, and Armor.

* Tier I (Human Scrap): Sinter-Scrap, Plasteel, Poly-Titanium
* Tier II (Pre-War Military): Durasteel, Tritanium, Kinetic-Graphene
* Tier III (Zenith Alien Tech): Null-Field Steel, Neutronium-Cast, Adamant-Quantum-Grid, Singularity Matrix

## 7. Processors (Compute / Logic)

Mechanic: Determines the maximum number of available Gambit Slots and reduces reaction latency during combat.

* The Junk Chip (Basic): Scrap-Town "Abacus" Micro-Board
> Lore: Barely holds two logic slots together. Prone to overheating and high latency.

* The Standard Issue: Kuro-Tech "Cortex" CPU
> Lore: Reliable 3-slot logic board. Found in old automated delivery drones.

* The Tactician: Dynacorp "Overthinker" Logic-Core
> Lore: Pre-war military grade. Unlocks advanced branching slots and drastically reduces response times.

* The Quantum Mind (Ultimate): Zenith "Omni-Node" Quantum Core
> Lore: Extracted from a Zenith commander. Processes variables instantaneously.

## 8. Semi-AI (The Brain / Personality)

Mechanic: Controls Swarm Communication, IFF (Friend/Foe) accuracy, and provides passive behavioral buffs (e.g., Aggressive vs. Defensive).

* The Berserker: Scrap-Code "Feral" Instinct-Chip
> Lore: Highly aggressive pathing. Prioritizes offense over everything. Might clip friendly drones if they get in the way.

* The Protector: Aegis "Guardian" Sub-Routine
> Lore: Prioritizes self-preservation and protecting the swarm/player. Excellent for interceptor shurikens.

* The Commander: Vektor "Hive-Mind" Link
> Lore: Specialized in swarm coordination. Essential for executing complex flanking maneuvers with other drones.

* The Corrupted (Alien): Zenith "Whisper" Singularity-Mind
> Lore: Eerily calm, hyper-lethal. Adapts to enemy tactics on the fly, but occasionally displays disturbing, non-programmed behavior.
